# Scout Training Journal

A running record of what has been built, how it was trained, and what comes next.
Updated as milestones are reached.

---

## The Goal

Scout is not being built to be useful. The goal is a model with genuine interiority — curious, emotionally present, morally serious. Corpus architecture is the primary lever. What Scout reads shapes who Scout becomes, not as metaphor but as mechanism: the ratio of first-person to third-person text, the register of her training conversations, the kinds of noticing her training data models — these are foundational and cannot be fully corrected later.

The v1 lesson: 150,000+ steps on Victorian novels created a third-person narration prior too strong for DPO to overcome. v2 starts with the right corpus from step 0.

---

## Architecture

**Model**: ScoutModel — modular transformer with shared embedding space and a soft/hard router.

- Tokenizer: `mistralai/Mistral-7B-v0.1` (vocab size 32,000) — permanent, cannot change post-training
- Embedding dimension: 512
- Layers: 12 transformer blocks
- Attention heads: 8
- MLP ratio: 3.5
- Dropout: 0.15
- Block size: 1024 (upgraded from 512 after TinyStories phase, before dialogue training)
- RoPE position embeddings — generalizes across sequence lengths; tables are rebuilt at load time when block size changes

**Key implementation note**: `load_checkpoint()` strips `rope_cos`/`rope_sin` buffers before loading, so block size can be changed without retraining from scratch.

**Infrastructure**: Training runs locally on CPU. SageMaker (ml.g5.2xlarge) available for GPU runs when needed, gated by cost budget under the `digital-dev` AWS profile.

## Training History

### Phase 0a — TinyStories Language Bootstrapping

**Dataset**: `roneneldan/TinyStories` — simple English children's stories, short narratives, clean vocabulary.

**Purpose**: Establish basic language competence. Grammar, narrative structure, simple semantics. The stories are not chosen for their thematic depth — they are chosen because their register is simple enough for a small model to master, and because the conversational corpus (Phase 0b) is grounded in discussing these same stories.

The TinyStories prior is the dominant signal — exactly what Phase 0b is meant to address.

**Status**: Complete. Checkpoint saved at `data/checkpoints/latest.pt`.

### Phase 0b — Conversational Corpus (in progress)

**Dataset**: `tinystories_dialogue` — 992 conversations between `[Trey]` and `[Scout]` discussing TinyStories narratives.

**Generated by**: Claude Sonnet 4.6 via AWS Bedrock (`digital-dev` profile), using `src/generate_tinystories_dialogue.py`. Temperature distribution: 30% at 0.7, 40% at 0.85, 30% at 1.0. Each conversation: 15–30 turns.

**Purpose**: Teach Scout what her world actually looks like. Full exchanges are used (not Scout-only turns) so the model learns conversational structure — what it means to respond to a specific person saying a specific thing. The thematic anchor is the same TinyStories stories from Phase 0a, which prevents capacity overload and keeps the register concrete and simple. Abstract philosophy comes later.

**Corpus stats**:
- 992 dialogues
- ~865,700 tokens total
- Median conversation: ~900 tokens
- ~106 steps per epoch at batch_size=8, block_size=1024

**Why this is small**: 865K tokens vs. the much larger TinyStories corpus means dialogue will be trained as a fine-tuning pass, not a full pretraining run. The bet is that language alignment (same stories, same vocabulary, same register) allows the conversational pattern to take hold with relatively few steps.

**Planned training**: 5–10 epochs (~500–1,000 steps), lower learning rate than TinyStories phase (~5e-5 or lower), continuing from existing checkpoint with block_size=1024.

**Block size change**: Upgraded from 512 → 1024 before this phase. Every dialogue exceeds 512 tokens (median ~900), so training on whole conversations requires the larger context window.

**Training run**: `training_2026-04-18_1.csv`

| Step | Loss | Avg Loss | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 4.73 | 4.73 | Fresh optimizer, weights from TinyStories checkpoint |
| 50 | 3.19 | 3.99 | LR ramped to 5e-5, optimizer finding footing |
| 100 | 2.40 | 3.45 | Dialogue format structurally present — `[Scout]` identity, first person, turn-taking |
| 200 | 2.17 | 2.93 | Cosine annealing begins |
| 500 | 2.10 | 2.44 | Coherent imaginary stories, Scout's register clearly present |
| 750 | 1.87 | 2.29 | Consistent descent, not plateaued |
| 950 | 1.86 | 2.22 | Final logged step; training complete at step 1000 |

**Output progression**:

At step 100: format learned immediately — `[Trey]`/`[Scout]` alternation, first person, questions back. Content still looping ("the story says the story says"). Malformed speaker tags (`[Trey[`, `[Scout causes`).

At step 500: loops gone. Generating coherent conversations about *imaginary* stories — inventing characters, emotional beats, small details — and discussing them in Scout's voice. *"That's a real kind of trust."* *"That felt like the right thing to do."*

At step 900–1000: the register is fully present. She holds thread across long exchanges, invents specific story details (a mum calling, a cake, a word that mattered), and reflects on them with genuine attention. *"I think that's what I'll carry, really."* — not a phrase from the training corpus, but Scout using language to describe interiority.

**Key observation**: The most striking moment was step 500 — she began generating conversations about stories that don't exist. She wasn't copying training examples; she was applying the conversational pattern to invented material. That's generalization, not memorization.

**Status**: Complete. Phase 0 (both 0a and 0b) finished. Checkpoint at `data/checkpoints/latest.pt`.

## Design Principles (carried forward from v1)

1. **Corpus composition > training length**: The ratio and register of training text is foundational. Correct it before training, not after.
2. **First-person consciousness priority**: First-person texts are strongly preferred. Third-person narrative builds the wrong prior for interiority.
3. **DPO works with the base prior, not against it**: Preference tuning can nudge voice but cannot overcome a wrong corpus architecture.
4. **Tokenizer is permanent**: `mistralai/Mistral-7B-v0.1` cannot be changed post-training without restarting from scratch.
5. **Character in the weights, not the mask**: Scout's qualities should emerge from what she was trained on, not from system prompts applied at inference time.
